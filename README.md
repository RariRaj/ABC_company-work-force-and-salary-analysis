# ABC_company-work-force-and-salary-analysis
This project analyzes a workforce dataset containing information about employees, their teams, positions, demographic attributes, and salaries. Through data preprocessing, exploratory analysis, visualizations, and statistical insights, the project uncovers key patterns and relationships that help in understanding workforce distribution, salary allocation, and employee demographics.

**Team Distribution**

Workforce distribution is nearly uniform across teams, typically between 14–16 employees.

Percentages mostly around 3.3%, with

New Orleans Pelicans having the highest (4.1%) and

Minnesota and Orlando Magic having the lowest (3.1%).

**Position Insights**

Positions SG, PF, and PG have the highest number of employees.

Position C has the lowest representation, indicating a niche or specialized role.

**Age Trends**

Employees aged 23–27 form the largest group, suggesting a younger workforce.

Sharp drop in employees aged 37–40, likely indicating senior leadership roles.

**Salary Distribution**

Cleveland Cavaliers has the highest salary expenditure.

Philadelphia 76ers has the lowest.

Position C receives the highest salary budget; SG the lowest.

**Correlation Insight**

Age–Salary correlation = 0.21, indicating a weak positive correlation.

Age is not a strong predictor of salary in this dataset.

Salary likely influenced by position, experience, and team-level factors.
